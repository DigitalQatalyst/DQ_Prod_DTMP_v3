1. DTMP PLATFORM CONTEXT
What is DTMP?
The Digital Transformation Metamodel Platform (DTMP) is an internal enterprise platform designed to accelerate an organization's digital transformation journey toward becoming a Digital-Centric Organization (DCO).
DTMP provides:
â€¢	9 specialized marketplaces for transformation resources and services
â€¢	AI-powered intelligence and analytics across the digital estate
â€¢	Governance frameworks aligned to the 4D Model (Discern, Design, Deploy, Drive)
â€¢	Role-based access for different stakeholder groups (Staff, Managers, Directors, Executives, Partners)
Platform Architecture
DTMP operates in two stages:
Stage 1: Marketplaces (Public/Pre-login)
â€¢	Browse and discover available services and resources
â€¢	Explanatory content to help users understand what's available
â€¢	Search and filter capabilities
â€¢	Call-to-action to access detailed functionality
Stage 2: Service Hub (Authenticated/Post-login)
â€¢	Access detailed dashboards, tools, and workspaces
â€¢	Execute on selected services
â€¢	Collaborate with Transformation Office
â€¢	Role-based access to data and capabilities
â€¢	Layout: LVE (Left-Vertical-Expanded) 
o	Column 1: Sidebar navigation (all marketplaces)
o	Column 2: Resource/content list or categories
o	Column 3: Detailed content viewer or workspace
The 9 Marketplaces
1.	Learning Center - Courses, certifications, training materials
2.	Knowledge Center â† THIS MARKETPLACE

7.	Solutions Build - Request delivery of pre-built or custom solutions
8.	Lifecycle Management - Active transformation initiatives (retire, modernize, replace)

2. THIS MARKETPLACE: KNOWLEDGE CENTER
What is Knowledge Center?
Knowledge Center is the central repository of transformation knowledge, documentation, and best practices for the organization. It serves as the collective wisdom accumulated from transformation initiatives, industry research, and organizational experience.
Core Function: Provide searchable, categorized access to articles, guides, case studies, reference materials, FAQs, lessons learned, and best practices that help users navigate their digital transformation journey.
Why Does This Marketplace Exist?
Problem Being Solved:
Organizations typically struggle with:
â€¢	âŒ Knowledge scattered - Documentation spread across wikis, SharePoint, email, personal drives
â€¢	âŒ Tribal knowledge - Critical information exists only in people's heads
â€¢	âŒ Reinventing the wheel - Teams solve problems that others have already solved
â€¢	âŒ Lost lessons learned - Insights from past projects are not captured or shared
â€¢	âŒ No single source - Users don't know where to find transformation guidance
â€¢	âŒ Outdated content - No clear ownership or review process for documentation
â€¢	âŒ Low discoverability - Good content exists but can't be found when needed
Solution:
Knowledge Center provides:
â€¢	âœ… Centralized repository - Single place for all transformation knowledge
â€¢	âœ… Curated content - Transformation Office reviews and maintains quality
â€¢	âœ… Searchable library - Find answers quickly with search and filters
â€¢	âœ… Categorized by topic - Browse by transformation phase, domain, or content type
â€¢	âœ… Living documentation - Regularly updated with new learnings
â€¢	âœ… Best practices - Proven approaches from successful initiatives
â€¢	âœ… Self-service - Users find answers without asking for help
â€¢	âœ… Contribution model - Teams can share their knowledge back
Knowledge Center vs. Learning Center
These are different marketplaces serving different needs:
Dimension	Knowledge Center	Learning Center
Purpose	Reference & documentation	Structured education
Content Type	Articles, guides, wikis	Courses, certifications
Format	Read-at-your-own-pace	Structured lessons + assessments
Learning Model	Just-in-time, need-based	Comprehensive, sequential
Completion	No completion tracking	Progress tracking, certificates
Use Case	"How do I solve X?"	"Teach me about Y"
Example	"API Gateway Design Guide"	"API Design Course (8 hours)"
Analogy	Stack Overflow, Wikipedia	Udemy, Coursera
When to use Knowledge Center:
â€¢	"I need to quickly reference the API naming conventions"
â€¢	"What's the best practice for data migration?"
â€¢	"Has anyone solved this integration problem before?"
When to use Learning Center:
â€¢	"I want to learn cloud architecture fundamentals"
â€¢	"I need certification in SAFe methodology"
â€¢	"Train my team on microservices patterns"

### **Key RBAC Principles**

**Open Read Access:**
- All published articles visible to all authenticated users
- Promotes knowledge sharing culture
- No content filtering by role

**Controlled Write Access:**
- Anyone can contribute articles
- Only TO can publish (quality control)
- Authors can edit their own drafts
- TO can edit any article

**Draft Privacy:**
- Only author + TO can see drafts
- Prevents premature exposure of incomplete work

**Contribution Tracking:**
- All contributions attributed to author
- Builds knowledge-sharing culture
- Recognition for contributors
